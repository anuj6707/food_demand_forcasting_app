Dataset Overview

  • Total Records: 456,548
  • Unique Fulfillment Centers: 77
  • Unique Meal Types: 51
  • Prediction Target: Number of Food Orders

This dataset contains historical food demand information collected across multiple fulfillment centers. The objective is to predict the number of food orders using pricing, promotions and meal information.

Effect of Discounts on Demand

The analysis shows that promotional discounts generally increase food demand. Lower checkout prices tend to generate more customer orders, although demand also depends on the meal category and fulfillment center.

Weekly Demand Trend

Customer demand changes throughout the weeks rather than remaining constant. This indicates that demand forecasting requires historical patterns instead of relying solely on price.

Correlation Heatmap

The heatmap illustrates relationships between numerical variables.Pricing variables exhibit strong relationships while promotional variables contribute additional predictive information.

Feature Importance

The trained XGBoost model identifies pricing, promotional discounts,fulfillment center and meal category as the most influential features for predicting food demand.

Key Findings

  • Promotional discounts generally increase customer demand.
  • Meal category significantly affects the number of expected orders.
  • Different fulfillment centers exhibit different ordering patterns.
  • Pricing variables contribute strongly to demand prediction.
  • Combining pricing, promotions and historical information enables accurate forecasting.
